# mf-tool-001 outcome reference

Stable IDs for agent workflows. Full detail in `rules.json` → `outcomes`.

## manual_ok

Stay manual. Low frequency or low stakes.

**Do:** laminated checklist, calendar revisit, 30-day time log.  
**Do not:** SaaS or dev spend until pain is measured.

## template

Template or checklist before software. Process repeats but is not stable.

**Do:** one canonical script, 30-day unchanged trial, note exceptions.  
**Do not:** custom app until template survives busy week.  
**See:** mf-tpl-001 for customer comms.

## spreadsheet

Shared sheet is enough. Tabular, multi-person visibility.

**Do:** one Sheet, one structure owner, formulas before APIs.  
**Do not:** tool until permissions/mobile/10+ daily editors break the sheet.

## simple_tool

Existing SaaS fits. Known category (forms, scheduling, docs).

**Do:** must-have list, 14-day real-data trial, export test.  
**Do not:** custom build without 80% SaaS coverage search.

## build

Custom build may be justified. High volume, stable spec, clear ROI.

**Do:** one-page spec, 2-week smallest slice, measure hours saved.  
**Do not:** greenfield before sheet + template failed.

## dont_automate

Humans in the loop. Judgment, empathy, safety every time.

**Do:** training, scripts, templates for logging only.  
**Do not:** AI auto-replies, unattended customer workflows.  
**See:** mf-tpl-001.

## gather_data

Not enough data. Automating a guess wastes money.

**Do:** 90-day log (date, who, minutes, errors), then re-run rubric.  
**Do not:** vendor quotes before monthly volume is known.
